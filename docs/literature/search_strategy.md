# Literature Search Strategy

A documented, reproducible search strategy is the foundation of a credible literature review. It establishes that the review is systematic rather than selective — that the papers cited were not chosen because they support a predetermined conclusion.

---

## Search Objectives

What the literature review must establish:
1. What methods have been applied to similar problems?
2. What findings exist that this project's hypotheses must be consistent or inconsistent with?
3. Where is the gap — the specific absence of knowledge — that this project fills?

A literature review that cannot identify a specific gap does not justify a new study.
